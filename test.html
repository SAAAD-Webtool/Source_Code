Lipinski's Rule of Five

Lipinski's Rule of Five is a series of criteria used in medicinal chemistry to assess the drug-likeness of compounds, particularly their potential for orally active usage. The rule is based on the observation that most orally active medicines have specific chemical features that are essential for absorption and permeability in the human body. These properties are:

1. Molecular weight (MW):
  • Should be less than 500 Daltons.
  • Larger molecules are generally poorly absorbed.
2. Log P (Partition Coefficient):
  • Should be less than five.
  • Determines how effectively the substance dissolves in fat versus water. High numbers indicate low water solubility and absorption.
3. Hydrogen Bond Donor (HBD):
  • Should be no more than five.
  • These are usually OH or NH groups. Too many can make it difficult for the molecule to pass through cell membranes.
4. Hydrogen bond acceptors (HBAs):
  • Should be no more than ten.
  • These are usually N or O atoms. Too many can make it difficult for the molecule to pass through cell membranes.
What is SAAAD?

The acronym SAAAD stands for "Sialic Acid Analogues ADME Prediction Database." It is a specific database that contains information about several sialic acid analogues, such as SMILES, formula, and ADME properties. This database is valuable for research and academic purposes, specifically in the field of medicinal chemistry and drug design.

What are ADME properties?

ADME stands for Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, and Excretion. These properties are crucial in determining the pharmacokinetics of a compound, that is, how it is absorbed, distributed in the body, metabolized, and eventually excreted. In drug development, understanding ADME properties is essential to predict the behavior of the compound in a biological system.

How can I search for specific sialic acid analogues in the database?

To search for specific sialic acid analogues, go to the "Search" tab on the website. In the search box, type the name or any identifying information about the analogue you are looking for. The system will provide suggestions based on your input. Click on the search button to see the results.

Can I download information about sialic acid analogues?

Yes, you can download information about specific sialic acid analogues. After searching for the analogue, the results will display an option to download the corresponding .mol file. Click on the download buttton get the file.

Who can benefit from using the SAAAD?

SAAAD is designed for researchers, scientists, and students in the fields of medicinal chemistry, biochemistry, pharmacology, and related areas. It is a valuable resource for those involved in drug design and discovery, as well as those studying the biological roles of sialic acid analogues.

How can I contribute to the SAAAD?

If you have valuable information or data related to sialic acid analogues that you would like to share, please contact us through the "Contact Us" tab. We welcome contributions from researchers and scientists to enhance the database's content.

Is there any cost associated with using the SAAAD?

No, accessing and using the SAAAD is completely free of charge. It is a publicly available resource for the scientific community.

Can I suggest improvements or report issues with the database?

Yes, we appreciate feedback from users. If you have suggestions for improvements or encounter any issues while using the SAAAD, please let us know through the "Contact Us" tab. Your feedback helps us enhance the functionality and content of the database.
