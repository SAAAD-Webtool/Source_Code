About SAAAD

SAAAD is a comprehensive resource on sialic acid analogues and their ADME (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, and Excretion) properties. It includes 155 entries containing SMILES, molecular formulas, hydrogen bond acceptors, donors, and Lipinski's rule information to aid in your research.

Explore Our Database

Search for specific sialic acid equivalents easily and access a wide range of data. Display and download sialic acid analogues to use in your studies. The database provides rich information to assist researchers in understanding sialic acid and its derivatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is SAAAD?

The acronym SAAAD stands for "Sialic Acid Analogues ADME Prediction Database." It is a specialized database containing critical information about sialic acid analogues, including SMILES, formula, and ADME predictions. This resource is invaluable for researchers in medicinal chemistry and drug design.

What are ADME predictions?

ADME stands for Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, and Excretion. These predictions help determine how a compound behaves in the body, including its absorption, distribution, metabolism, and eventual excretion. ADME predictions are a cornerstone in drug development.

How can I search for specific sialic acid analogues in the database?

Navigate to the "Search" tab on the website. Enter the name or key details of the analogue in the search box, and the system will provide suggestions and results based on your input.

Can I download information about sialic acid analogues?

Yes! After finding the analogue, you will see a download option next to the results. Simply click on it to download the corresponding .mol file for your reference.
